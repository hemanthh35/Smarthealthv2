6 Exercises to Tone Every Inch of Your Body

1. Squats

Target Muscles: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, lower back, core.

Benefits: Improves leg strength, balance, and mobility; activates the core for stability.

How to Perform:

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Keep your chest up and lower your hips as if sitting on a chair.
  • Go down until thighs are parallel to the floor (or lower if possible).
  • Push through your heels to return to the starting position.

Reps/Sets: 3–4 sets of 10–15 reps.

2. Lunges

Target Muscles: Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves, core.

Benefits: Builds strength and stability in the legs; improves balance and coordination.

How to Perform:

  • Step forward with one leg while keeping your torso upright.
  • Lower your body until both knees form a 90-degree angle.
  • Push through the front heel to return to the starting position.
  • Alternate legs for each repetition.

Reps/Sets: 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps per leg.

3. Planks

Target Muscles: Core (abs, obliques), shoulders, back.

Benefits: Strengthens core stability, improves posture, and supports lower back health.

How to Perform:

  • Get into a push-up position with forearms on the ground.
  • Keep your body straight from head to heels.
  • Engage your core and hold the position.

Hold Time: 3–4 sets of 20–60 seconds.

4. Push-Ups

Target Muscles: Chest, shoulders, triceps, core.

Benefits: Improves upper body strength and engages the core for stability.

How to Perform:

  • Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Lower your body until your chest is just above the floor.
  • Push back up to the starting position.

Reps/Sets: 3–4 sets of 10–20 reps (modify with knees on the ground if needed).

5. Pull-Ups

Target Muscles: Biceps, triceps, forearms, shoulders, core.

Benefits: Builds upper body strength and grip strength.

How to Perform:

  • Grab a pull-up bar with an overhand or underhand grip.
  • Pull your body upward until your chin clears the bar.
  • Lower yourself slowly.

Reps/Sets: 3–4 sets of 5–10 reps (use a resistance band or assistance if needed).

6. Shoulder Presses

Target Muscles: Shoulders, core, upper back.

Benefits: Strengthens the deltoids, improves posture, and builds shoulder stability.

How to Perform:

  • Hold dumbbells or a barbell at shoulder height.
  • Press the weight overhead until arms are fully extended.
  • Slowly return to the starting position.

Reps/Sets: 3–4 sets of 8–12 reps.

Workout Routine Suggestion

  • Day 1: Squats, Lunges, Planks
  • Day 2: Push-Ups, Pull-Ups, Shoulder Presses
  • Day 3: Rest or light cardio/stretching

Repeat: Alternate days, or perform all exercises on the same day for a full-body routine.
